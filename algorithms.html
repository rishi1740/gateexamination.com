Algorithms   >    

This section carries nearly 10-15% weight age in the GATE exam.

Questions from this section are mostly conceptual therefore focus on getting thorough with the concepts.

CLRS Introduction to Algorithm is best for Gate and other product based companies interviews,programming interviews even Google interviews question are from this book.
Many questions directly appears from the coreman

You need to be fully focussed while reading this books concentration is the key.

Many says its the Programmers Bible so you should be ready to spend a day only reading it but dont go in deep read specific topic that are in gate syllabus.
Topics are as follows:
Chapter 1 to 3 is important
Ch-4 is important(except section 4.4)
Ch-6 to 7(except section 7.3)is important
Ch-8 to 10 is important
Ch-11(except 11.5)is important
Ch-12(except section 12.4)is important
Ch-18is important
Ch-22 to 24(except 24.4 and 24.5)is important
Asymptotic worst case time and space complexity.Graph search, minimum spanning trees, shortest paths.

Do exercises from Cormen
Cormen solution manual is available over the internet download it and first try to solve problem on your own rather than seeing solutions.

Lecture videos of Prof. Naveen Garg of IITD is also good .

MIT lectures given by Liesersonone one of the authors of CLRS and Erik demaine.

Algorithm is advised to be read after you had read discrete mathematics or along with it for better understanding of the subject.

Stack push and pop, Queues singly ,doubly ,circular , Linked List can be in form of a C program. Implement stack using linked list .Defination ,properties and operations such as insertion and deletion of heaps.

Solve Tree problems i.e. no of leaf nodes, non leaf nodes, total number of nodes, height of tree, number of full nodes. AVL tree and balancing them on insertion and Deletion.
Binary tree, Binary Search Tree, Inorder, Preorder, Postorder traversal. Spanning Trees, Minimum Spanning Tree problems
best case, average case, and worst case complexities.

You should be able to find out complexitiy of each algorithm or or program (take the help of internet).Read in details property of complexities.

Divide and Conquer, Greedy and Brute Force are important topics for algorithm design. Practice basic problems like quick sort, merge sort, knapsack problem, matrix chain multiplication, LCS, Job sequencing, Compressing Mechanism.

Know about data types elemental(stack, queues, and bags), sorting algorithms (quicksort, mergesort, heapsort), data structures (binary search trees, red-black trees, hash tables)
big-0 complexity Arrays, stacks, queues, linked lists, trees, binary search trees, binary heaps, graphs.

Complexity is important topic.
Direct questions based on complexity like complexity of quick sort. There would be a question and you would have to find
the best or average case of that problem and also practice relation between complexity.

Searching and sorting problems and difference between them and
how to implement them in practical life as well.

To answer questions from the subject Algorithms in quick time, you need to have good understanding of design techniques.
Design techniques, Master Theorem, and Asymptotic notations
should be clear to you as it is very important.

Sorting algorithms has maximum weightage in previous year gate question
